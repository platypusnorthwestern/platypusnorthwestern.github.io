About Platypus

Who we are, and what we've inherited.

The Platypus Affiliated Society, established in December 2006, organizes reading groups, public fora, research and journalism focused on problems and tasks inherited from the “Old” (1920s–30s), “New” (1960s–70s) and post-political (1980s–90s) Left for the possibilities of emancipatory politics today.

The Northwestern University chapter hosts a weekly reading group, public panels, teach-ins, and workshops, including sessions of the annual Platypus International Convention. We approach the history of the Left not as a settled inheritance but as an open problem — one that has to be worked through, not simply cited, if emancipatory politics is to become possible again.
